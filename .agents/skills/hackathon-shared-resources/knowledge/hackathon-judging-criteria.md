# Hackathon Judging Criteria

Standard judging dimensions used across major hackathons. Actual weights vary per event; use `hackathon-track-analyzer` to extract event-specific weights.

---

## Core Dimensions

### 1. Technical Complexity & Implementation

**What judges assess:** Depth of engineering, use of relevant APIs/tools, quality of working demo.

- Is the hard part technically hard?
- Does it work live during the demo?
- Is the tech stack appropriate for the problem?
- Bonus: novel use of sponsor APIs or provided datasets.

**Common weight range:** 20–35% of total score.

---

### 2. Innovation & Originality

**What judges assess:** How new is the idea? How creatively is it executed?

- Does this solve the problem in a meaningfully different way?
- Is there a novel mechanism, not just a new wrapper?
- Have other teams attempted similar solutions?

**Common weight range:** 15–30% of total score.

---

### 3. Impact & Potential

**What judges assess:** How large is the potential benefit if this scales?

- How many people does this affect?
- How severe is the problem being solved?
- Is there a credible path to real-world deployment?

**Common weight range:** 15–25% of total score.

---

### 4. Design & User Experience

**What judges assess:** Usability, visual polish, and accessibility.

- Can a non-technical user understand the product in 30 seconds?
- Does the UI/UX reflect empathy for the target user?
- Is the demo experience smooth and glitch-free?

**Common weight range:** 10–20% of total score.

---

### 5. Pitch & Presentation

**What judges assess:** Clarity, structure, confidence, and story.

- Is the problem clearly communicated?
- Does the solution follow logically from the problem?
- Is the team composed and persuasive under questioning?

**Common weight range:** 15–25% of total score.

---

### 6. Track / Sponsor Alignment

**What judges assess:** Whether the project addresses the sponsor's challenge.

- Does the project use the sponsor's technology/API/dataset?
- Does it address the stated theme of the prize track?
- Is the sponsor's language used in the pitch?

**Common weight range:** Variable; often decides prize track winners.

---

## Common Judging Formats

| Format      | Description                                             |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Expo Table  | Judges rotate through team tables; 3–5 min per team     |
| Stage Pitch | Teams pitch to panel; 2–5 min + Q&A                     |
| Video Only  | Async judging from submitted video and description      |
| Hybrid      | Expo round narrows to finalists who then pitch on stage |

---

## Judge Composition Patterns

- **Technical judges:** Prioritize working code and novel implementation.
- **Business judges:** Prioritize market size and monetization path.
- **Domain experts:** Prioritize accuracy and real-world fit.
- **Sponsor reps:** Prioritize use of their technology.

Calibrate pitch emphasis to judge composition when known.
